Agate at Night

Experiment 2 - Vector Art, Animation, and Interactivity

Imitate

When I was browsing through the Generative Design examples looking for inspiration for this project, the following two examples stood out to me.

Example 1

Example 2

I liked these images because they immediately reminded me of the patterns in sliced agate stones, which I think are really pretty. I decided that for my project I wanted to try to use sliced agate stone as my inspiration. I then went searching for some images of agate patterns and colors, which I have included below.

Integrate

Since one of the examples I was working from included shapes I liked, and the other included a way of arranging them that I liked, I began by trying to find a way to combine those two concepts.

I decided to try modifying the example with the shape that I liked. First, I unlinked the center point of the shape from the mouse, and made it so it was centered in the middle of the page.

Then, I started testing ways to make it get smaller with each addition. My first attempt was to modify the range of the amount added to the x and y calculations for the points, but that didn’t work—it just started moving the shape downward.

I then tried adding a multiplier to reduce the step size with each iteration, but that didn’t work either, though it did look cool!

Finally, I figured out how to do it, by checking to see which side of the center each point is on.

Unfortunately, it seemed to have some trouble as points approach the x and y axis. After some modifications to a few of the variables, I finally ended up with a result I was happy with.I decided to add back in the option to click to create another shape, since I didn’t end up including the interactivity for the changing sizes. Plus, I thought it would turn out interestingly in the next step if the user can create more than one of the agate stones.

Innovate

The shapes were looking good at this point, but they didn’t have color yet. First, I added random colors. Unfortunately, those looked more like strange candies than like agate stones. So I had to limit the color palettes.

I tried a green, and then an orange-red. I liked the orange-red, but wanted to get more creative. I ended up modifying the orange-red one to feel a little more natural, and added a green and blue option. The colors are randomized per stone. It still looked a little off to me though.

I removed the strokes, and I think the result looks much better. I changed the background color to black to make the colors stand out more. At this point, I’m happy with the results. I feel like the shapes do look like agate stones, and the finished results feel very different from the examples I started with.
